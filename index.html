Study Up to Lock Your Info In

Companies collect personal data about you - like your interests, locations, emails, and more.

When you use apps, websites, or other services, companies routinely collect personal information about you without explicit consent. Data privacy refers to the appropriate protection and use of your information, giving you rights and control over how it is accessed and managed.

  • Poor privacy practices mean your data could be used to harm or discriminate against you behind the scenes.
  • Being aware of privacy policies, using strong passwords, and recognizing scam emails are some ways you can start to better protect your information.
  • Data privacy affects you every day, so pay attention to how companies handle your personal information.

Why Is Protecting Your Data Important?

Protecting your data is crucial as these things could happen if you don't.

Identity Theft

If hackers steal your personal data like social security numbers, birth dates, etc they can steal your identity and open fraudulent accounts or make purchases in your name, ruining your credit.

Financial Loss

Hackers can drain your financial accounts, make unauthorized transactions with payment information, or steal money. The average victim loses around $2000.

Reputational Damage

If sensitive business data, private messages or photos are leaked, it can hurt your professional reputation or personal relationships.

Blackmail

Criminals can threaten to publicly expose private data or make other blackmail demands if you don't meet payment requests.

Stalking/Harassment

Access to contact details and location history leaves people vulnerable to digital stalking, bullying and persistent harassment.

Emotional Distress

Having private data exposed can create immense personal stress, anxiety, loss of feeling safe, and other emotional trauma with long lasting effects.

How Can You Protect Your Data?

Every day, we put more of our personal information at potential risk online. However, taking a few key steps can keep your data safe.

Secure Accounts

Empower account security with strong unique passwords, 2-factor authentication, and a password manager.

Limit Tracking

Disable location tracking and opt-out of data collection from ads and personalization wherever possible.

VPNs & Encryption

Utilize encrypted VPN connections over public WiFi and enable privacy settings across accounts and devices by default.

Back Up Data

Leverage encrypted local and cloud backups that shield important personal data from loss events like ransomware while vetting provider security postures.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How can I tell if a website or app properly secures my data?

    Look for indicators like HTTPS connections, the presence of a privacy policy that clearly explains data practices, and whether the company has undergone a data security audit. Reputable services will make data protections easy to find and understand.

  • What's the difference between encryption and hashing for data security?

    Encryption scrambles data so only authorized parties can read it. Hashing creates a unique fingerprint from data that can't be reversed. Both make data more secure. Encryption keeps data private; hashing verifies integrity and authenticity.

  • What are cookies and how do they impact privacy?

    Cookies are small text files websites place on your device to store browsing data. They can track online activity to show relevant ads or remember login credentials. But malicious cookies can compromise privacy. Use cookie consent tools, private browsing, or cookie blockers.

  • How does a VPN help protect my online data?

    Virtual Private Networks route internet traffic through encrypted tunnels to keep data secure and anonymize browsing by hiding your IP address and location. This prevents snooping on public Wi-Fi and gets around geo-blocks. VPNs boost privacy but must be reputable to trust they don't misuse your data.

  • How long should companies retain personal data?

    There are varying data retention laws and guidelines. Generally, companies should only store customer data only as long as necessary to fulfill the original purpose it was collected for. Sensitive data should have shorter retention spans. Make sure companies properly dispose of old records per data protection best practices.
